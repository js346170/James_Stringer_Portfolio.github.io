Tsinghua International School Implementation

As part of the Social Studies Department at Tsinghua International School, I participated in the long-standing National History Day (NHD) program, a semester-long activity that engaged students in authentic historical research.

Grade-Level Activities

9th Grade Research Project

  • Utilized NoodleTools for source organization
  • Emphasized primary source analysis
  • Developed organizational skills with digital note cards
  • Created comprehensive final research products

AP US History Document Based Question Project

  • Students designed their own DBQs enhancing both historical understanding and critical knowledge of the test structure
  • Selected topics based on personal research interests
  • Practiced advanced research and analytical skills

History Day Event

The semester culminated in History Day, where students showcased their research projects to the entire school community as a gallery walk. This event celebrated student work and fostered a culture of academic excellence.

COVID-19 Adaptation

During my first year coordinating History Day, the in-person event was interrupted by COVID-19 restrictions. With only a few days notice, I:

  • Transitioned the entire event to a virtual format
  • Created digital showcases for student projects
  • Organized online presentation sessions
  • Maintained student engagement despite disruptions

The online History Day was a remarkable success, demonstrating flexibility and resilience in educational programming.

Bringing NHD to Delta Global School

Upon joining Delta Global School, I introduced National History Day to my 9th-grade social studies classes, adapting the successful model from Tsinghua International School.

Initial Implementation (2023-24)

Introduced the research project to 9th-grade classes with a focus on:

  • Source evaluation using NoodleTools
  • Thesis development and historical argumentation
  • Note card organization system

Convincing School Leadership

Successfully advocated for school-wide adoption of NoodleTools as an essential research platform.

Future Expansion Plans

Currently working to establish NHD as a whole-department activity across grade levels in coming school years.

Educational Impact

National History Day develops essential skills that extend beyond history classrooms:

Research Skills

Students learn to locate, evaluate, and synthesize information from diverse sources.

Critical Thinking

Analyzing historical evidence develops analytical reasoning and argumentation skills.

Project Management

Managing a semester-long project teaches organization and time management.

Communication

Presenting findings develops written and oral communication abilities.

Legacy

National History Day has become a transformative experience for students at both schools. The program develops not just historical knowledge, but essential research, critical thinking, and presentation skills that serve students in all academic disciplines. As it expands to more grade levels at Delta Global School, it will continue to foster a culture of academic inquiry and excellence.
