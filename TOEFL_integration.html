TOEFL Curriculum Reform

Led research-driven reforms that increased average TOEFL scores from 78 to 93 in two years

Key Achievement

As the TOEFL expert at Tianjin FLS, I spearheaded an action research project that transformed our English curriculum, resulting in a 19% increase in average TOEFL scores (78 to 93) while maintaining our focus on authentic language proficiency.

The Challenge

Our initial research revealed several critical issues:

  • Class grades showed little correlation with TOEFL scores for mid-performing students
  • Students were splitting focus between schoolwork and external TOEFL prep courses
  • Curriculum gaps existed in integrated tasks, writing variety, and cognitively demanding activities
  • Students didn't recognize connections between classroom activities and TOEFL skills

Research & Implementation

Initial Research Phase

Conducted correlation studies between GPA and TOEFL scores, identifying the "middle band" discrepancy. Led qualitative interviews revealing students' divided focus between school and external TOEFL prep.

December 2018 Report

Curriculum Alignment

Led vertical alignment of English curriculum from Year 1 to Year 3. Created detailed mapping of TOEFL skills/subskills and compared to our existing curriculum.

March 2019 Update

Key Interventions

  • Added integrated tasks combining reading/listening/writing
  • Expanded writing task variety to match TOEFL prompts
  • Incorporated more cognitively demanding activities
  • Marked TOEFL-relevant materials with ETS/SAT logos
  • Implemented cross-curricular vocabulary alignment
Shared Learning Notes

Assessment Reform

Moved TOEFL testing under academic oversight to ensure validity. Trained all teachers in proper TOEFL invigilation techniques and assessment methodologies.

Measurable Outcomes

Score Increase

Average TOEFL scores rose from 78 to 93 (19% improvement) within two years of implementation.

Curriculum Alignment

Identified and addressed 3 key areas of difference with TOEFL requirements: integrated tasks, writing variety, and cognitive difficulty.

Student Engagement

Marking materials with ETS logos increased student engagement by making TOEFL connections visible.

Teacher Development

Trained entire faculty in TOEFL assessment methodologies and curriculum alignment strategies.

Theoretical Foundation

Our approach was grounded in TOEFL's communicative competence framework, focusing on:

  • Contextualized language use (academic vs. daily life)
  • Task-based assessment design
  • Vertical alignment of reading subskills (finding information → basic comprehension → learning → integration)
  • Balancing test preparation with authentic language development
